## Why Seasonal Maintenance Matters

Most expensive home repairs start as cheap fixes that got ignored. A $5 tube of caulk prevents a $5,000 water damage claim. An hour cleaning gutters prevents a $2,000 foundation repair. This checklist keeps you ahead of the problems.

## Spring Checklist

Spring is inspection season. Winter took a beating on your house — now's the time to find and fix what it broke.

### Exterior
- **Walk the perimeter.** Look for cracks in the foundation, damaged siding, and gaps around windows and doors. Caulk any gaps you find.
- **Check the roof.** From the ground with binoculars, or carefully from a ladder. Look for missing, curled, or damaged shingles.
- **Clean gutters and downspouts.** Flush with a hose and make sure water flows freely. Downspouts should direct water at least 4 feet away from the foundation.
- **Inspect the grading.** Soil should slope away from your foundation on all sides. If it's settled toward the house, add soil to restore the slope.

### Interior
- **Replace furnace filter.** If you haven't changed it since fall, it's overdue.
- **Test smoke and carbon monoxide detectors.** Replace batteries even if they seem fine.
- **Check under sinks** for any signs of leaks or moisture. Look at supply lines and drain connections.
- **Run water in unused fixtures.** If you have a guest bathroom or basement sink that doesn't get used, run water for a minute to refill the P-trap and prevent sewer gas from entering.

## Summer Checklist

Summer is outdoor maintenance season. Take advantage of the good weather.

### Exterior
- **Inspect and clean the deck or patio.** Power wash if needed. Check for loose boards, popping nails, or rot.
- **Trim vegetation away from the house.** Keep bushes and tree branches at least 12 inches from siding. Trees touching the roof need to be cut back.
- **Check exterior paint and stain.** Touch up any areas where paint is peeling or wood is exposed. Bare wood absorbs moisture and rots quickly.
- **Service the air conditioner.** Clean or replace the filter. Clear debris from around the outdoor unit. Consider a professional tune-up every 2-3 years.

### Interior
- **Check the attic.** Look for signs of roof leaks, proper insulation, and adequate ventilation. If it's extremely hot and humid up there, ventilation may be inadequate.
- **Clean dryer vent.** Disconnect the dryer and vacuum the vent hose and exterior vent cap. Lint buildup is a fire hazard.
- **Test the garage door auto-reverse.** Place a 2x4 on the ground in the door's path. If the door doesn't reverse when it contacts the board, it needs adjustment.

## Fall Checklist

Fall is prep season. Everything you do now prevents winter problems.

### Exterior
- **Clean gutters again.** Leaves are the enemy. Do this after most leaves have fallen.
- **Disconnect and drain garden hoses.** Shut off exterior faucet valves from inside if you have them. A frozen hose bib can burst a pipe inside the wall.
- **Inspect weatherstripping** on all exterior doors. Replace if cracked, compressed, or missing. You should feel resistance when you close the door.
- **Caulk gaps around windows, doors, and penetrations.** Use exterior-grade silicone or polyurethane caulk.
- **Store outdoor furniture** or cover it. Clean it first.

### Interior
- **Replace furnace filter.** Starting heating season with a clean filter.
- **Have the furnace serviced.** A professional tune-up catches problems before the coldest day of the year.
- **Test your sump pump** by pouring a bucket of water into the pit. It should kick on and pump the water out.
- **Check window locks and latches.** Tighten any that are loose.
- **Reverse ceiling fans.** Set them to clockwise in winter to push warm air down from the ceiling.

## Winter Checklist

Winter is monitoring season. Less outdoor work, but don't neglect the basics.

### Exterior
- **Keep snow away from the foundation.** When shoveling, don't pile snow against the house.
- **Prevent ice dams.** If you see icicles forming along the eaves, you may have an insulation or ventilation problem in the attic.
- **Check for frost heaving.** Walkways and driveways that have shifted may be a trip hazard. Mark them for spring repair.

### Interior
- **Check for drafts.** On a windy day, hold a lit candle near windows and doors. If the flame flickers, you've got an air leak.
- **Monitor humidity levels.** Winter air is dry, which is hard on wood floors and furniture. But too much humidity causes condensation on windows. Aim for 30-50%.
- **Replace furnace filter.** Yes, again. Monthly during heavy use is ideal.
- **Know where your main water shut-off is.** If a pipe bursts, seconds count. Make sure every adult in the house knows the location.

## The 15-Minute Monthly Walk

Beyond the seasonal checklists, do a quick walk through your home once a month:

1. **Look under every sink** for drips or moisture
2. **Check the water heater** for leaks or rust
3. **Glance at the ceiling** in every room for stains or discoloration
4. **Test one smoke detector** (rotate through them)
5. **Check the sump pump** if you have one

Most problems announce themselves before they become emergencies. You just have to be looking.

## When to Call a Pro

- Furnace making unusual noises or not heating evenly
- Roof damage you can't safely reach
- Foundation cracks that are growing or wider than 1/4 inch
- Persistent musty smells (possible hidden mold)
- Electrical issues — flickering lights, warm outlets, tripping breakers
